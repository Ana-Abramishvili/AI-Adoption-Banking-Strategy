# AI Risk & Governance – Responsible AI Adoption in Banking

## Overview

This document outlines key risks, governance principles, and mitigation strategies for the adoption of Generative AI (GenAI) in banking operations.

The goal is to ensure that AI systems are deployed in a **safe, compliant, transparent, and responsible manner**, aligned with financial industry regulations and internal policies.

---

## 1. Key Risk Areas

### 1.1 Data Privacy Risk

AI systems may process sensitive customer or employee data.

**Risks:**
- Unauthorized access to personal data
- Data leakage through prompts or outputs
- Non-compliance with GDPR or banking regulations

**Mitigation:**
- Use anonymized or aggregated datasets
- Implement strict access controls (role-based access)
- Ensure encryption of sensitive data

---

### 1.2 Hallucination & Accuracy Risk

Generative AI may produce incorrect or misleading information.

**Risks:**
- Incorrect responses to customer queries
- Inaccurate internal recommendations
- Loss of trust in AI systems

**Mitigation:**
- Human-in-the-loop validation for critical use cases
- Restrict AI to verified knowledge bases
- Continuous monitoring and evaluation of outputs

---

### 1.3 Security Risk

AI systems may be exposed to malicious inputs or attacks.

**Risks:**
- Prompt injection attacks
- Data extraction attempts
- Unauthorized system manipulation

**Mitigation:**
- Input sanitization and filtering
- Secure API integrations
- Regular security audits and testing

---

### 1.4 Bias & Fairness Risk

AI systems may unintentionally reflect bias in data.

**Risks:**
- Unfair treatment of customer segments
- Biased recommendations or decisions
- Reputational risk for the organization

**Mitigation:**
- Regular bias testing and evaluation
- Diverse training datasets
- Transparent model evaluation processes

---

### 1.5 Compliance & Regulatory Risk

Banking is a highly regulated environment.

**Risks:**
- Violation of financial regulations
- Lack of auditability in AI decisions
- Improper use of customer data

**Mitigation:**
- Close collaboration with Legal, Risk, and Compliance teams
- Maintain full audit logs of AI interactions
- Ensure explainability of AI-driven decisions

---

## 2. Governance Framework

A responsible AI governance structure should include:

### 2.1 Stakeholders

- Data & AI Teams
- Business Units
- Compliance Department
- Risk Management Team
- Information Security Team

---

### 2.2 Governance Principles

- Transparency: AI decisions should be explainable
- Accountability: Clear ownership of AI systems
- Security: Strong protection of sensitive data
- Fairness: Avoid bias in outputs and recommendations
- Human Oversight: Humans remain in control of critical decisions

---

## 3. AI Lifecycle Controls

### Phase 1: Design
- Define use case and risk level
- Identify required controls

### Phase 2: Development
- Use approved datasets
- Validate model outputs

### Phase 3: Deployment
- Restrict access based on roles
- Implement monitoring systems

### Phase 4: Monitoring
- Track performance and anomalies
- Collect user feedback
- Continuously improve system

---

## 4. Final Note

Responsible AI adoption in banking requires a balance between innovation and control.

Strong governance ensures that AI delivers business value while maintaining **trust, compliance, and security** across all operations.
